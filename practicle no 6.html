Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Scientist | Teacher | People's President

About

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was one of India's greatest scientists and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to missile development.

Key Achievements

  • Led the development of India's Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Played a vital role in India's 1998 nuclear tests.
  • Authored inspirational books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds.
  • Worked tirelessly to inspire students and promote education.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture. His simplicity, humility, and dedication to the youth of India continue to inspire generations.
